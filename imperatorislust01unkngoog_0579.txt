Tit. X Of the exceptions to the old rule nemo alieno nomine agere
potest (Dig. 50. 17. 123) ‘pro populo’ refers to the actiones populares,
and ‘pro libertate’ includes manumission per vindictam, p. 107 supr.
Nothing further is known of the lex Hostilia. What is meant by ‘pro
tutela' in the text is much disputed3 Theophilus' explanation of it by
supposing a suit between two or more persons claiming a tutela is
generally rejected, and perhaps it may be right to understand it os
actions brought generally by the tutor in relation to the ward’s property.
